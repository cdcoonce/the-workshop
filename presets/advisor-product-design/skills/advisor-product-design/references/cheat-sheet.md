# Cheat Sheet

Always loaded. The heuristics needed in most reviews — full claims, boundary
conditions, and citations live in the linked packs.

- **Start every dashboard with the decision, not the data** — if you can't name what the viewer decides with it, it's a data dump (→ packs/product-discovery-outcomes.md)
- **Ask what job the artifact is hired for** — who opens this, when, and why beats any pixel critique (→ packs/product-discovery-outcomes.md)
- **Define success as a behavior change, not a delivery** — "shipped" is output; what does someone do differently? (→ packs/product-discovery-outcomes.md)
- **Cite heuristics by name, argue by consequence** — a named violation with no user-task consequence doesn't ship in a review (→ packs/ux-patterns-heuristics.md)
- **Never show a naked number** — every KPI needs a target, prior, trend, or state; show the difference the viewer needs (→ packs/ux-patterns-heuristics.md)
- **Always answer "as of when?"** — data apps must surface freshness and long-running status; stale-but-confident is the worst state (→ packs/ux-patterns-heuristics.md)
- **Make every click smell like its destination** — labels must carry information scent; users follow promise, not hope (→ packs/ux-patterns-heuristics.md)
- **Design zero-data, error, and loading states deliberately** — the states engineers skip are the states users judge (→ packs/ux-patterns-heuristics.md)
- **Build hierarchy with weight and color before adding sizes** — reach for another type size last, not first (→ packs/visual-design-fundamentals.md)
- **Group with whitespace before boxes** — proximity does the work of most borders and cards (→ packs/visual-design-fundamentals.md)
- **Hold WCAG 2 AA contrast as the shipping floor** — non-negotiable baseline, not a nice-to-have (→ packs/visual-design-fundamentals.md)
- **Enforce touch-target minimums as a hard gate** — undersized targets are a Blocker on mobile, not a Minor (→ packs/mobile-native-conventions.md)
- **Prefer undo over confirmation for routine deletes** — confirmation dialogs train blind clicking; undo preserves flow and safety (→ packs/mobile-native-conventions.md)
- **State what the AI can do and how well, at first contact** — capability and quality expectations are the first screen's job (→ packs/ai-product-ux.md)
- **Treat first-pass AI-generated UI as distribution-shaped, not designed** — it's the average of the training set until critiqued against your users (→ packs/design-tools-ecosystem.md)
